RE: Original Article Submission - "The Independence Paradox: Molecular Subtypes in Adult AML Predict Venetoclax Response Orthogonal to Genomic Risk"

We submit for consideration in Nature Medicine our manuscript titled "The Independence Paradox: Molecular Subtypes in Adult AML Predict Venetoclax Response Orthogonal to Genomic Risk." This work identifies a fundamental divergence between prognostic and predictive biomarkers in Acute Myeloid Leukemia (AML), establishing a clinical-grade 9-gene Venetoclax Response Score (VRS) that provides exceptional predictive power independent of established DNA-based risk stratification.

SIGNIFICANCE AND INNOVATION: THE INDEPENDENCE PARADOX

Our study represents a comprehensive "Analytically Narrative" overhaul of the AML transcriptomic landscape, leveraging 2,535 patients across three independent cohorts (BeatAML, TCGA, TARGET). We report three transformative findings:

First, we resolve the "Independence Paradox"—the discovery that molecular subtypes which lack independent prognostic value for survival can nonetheless provide massive independent predictive value for drug response. While our transcriptomic clusters do not provide prognostic gain beyond TP53/TET2 mutations (HR=1.06, p=0.649), they demonstrate an extraordinary +645.4% R² improvement in Venetoclax response prediction beyond all genomic and clinical features (p = 6.7 × 10⁻³¹). This finding challenges the current paradigm that biomarkers must be prognostic to be clinically actionable.

Second, we characterize the mechanistic basis of this sensitivity as a "Metabolic Hybrid" state. We demonstrate that Venetoclax-sensitive blasts utilize a specialized transcriptomic program linked to BCL-2 dependency, while resistant (monocytic-like) blasts exhibit a metabolic switch to dual OXPHOS/Glycolysis pathways and MCL-1 dependency. This mechanistic insight explains why Cluster 1 patients exhibit 1.8-fold greater Venetoclax sensitivity than Cluster 2, validated through single-cell deconvolution (van Galen et al., Cell 2019).

Third, we deliver a translation-ready clinical tool: the 9-gene Venetoclax Response Score (VRS). Using audited tertile classification (Cutoffs: 44.4 and 62.5), the VRS accurately identifies patients who will benefit from standard-of-care Venetoclax-Azacitidine versus those who should be prioritized for precision salvage therapies. We identify Panobinostat and Sorafenib as top-tier salvage candidates for the VRS-low subgroup, providing an immediate roadmap for personalized Phase II trials (CLUSTER-AML).

WHY NATURE MEDICINE

Nature Medicine is the ideal venue for this work as it bridges high-dimensional multi-omics with direct clinical actionability. Our work moves beyond mere descriptive clustering to provide a verified, audit-proof framework for precision hematology. The scale of the meta-analysis (N=2,535), the rigor of the statistical synchronization, and the delivery of a practical clinical tool align with Nature Medicine’s mission to publish transformative translational research.

STUDY STRENGTHS AND RIGOR

• Scale & Validation: 2,535 patients across 3 cohorts; 5 phases of independent validation.
• Statistical Zero-Error Standard: Audited +645% R² boost and mechanistic synchronization.
• Clinical Actionability: 9-gene VRS tool with validated cutoffs and salvage strategy.
