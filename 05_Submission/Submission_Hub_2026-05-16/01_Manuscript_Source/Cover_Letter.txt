RE: Original Article Submission - "Molecular Subtypes in Adult Acute Myeloid Leukemia Predict Venetoclax Response Independent of Genomic Alterations"

I am pleased to submit my Original Article titled "Molecular Subtypes in Adult Acute Myeloid Leukemia Predict Venetoclax Response Independent of Genomic Alterations" for consideration for publication in Blood.

Acute myeloid leukemia (AML) remains a cytogenetically and molecularly heterogeneous malignancy. While the approval of the selective BCL-2 inhibitor Venetoclax combined with hypomethylating agents (HMAs) or low-dose cytarabine has transformed non-intensive treatment paradigms, clinical response remains highly heterogeneous, and primary or acquired resistance represents an urgent clinical challenge. Current ELN guidelines rely exclusively on DNA mutation profiles (e.g., NPM1, FLT3, TP53) for risk stratification. However, whether transcriptomic lineage architecture captures therapeutic vulnerabilities that transcend genomic DNA alterations has remained a fundamental open question in clinical hematology.

In this study, we present a comprehensive multi-omics analysis encompassing >3,500 AML patients across 8 independent clinical, functional, and single-cell cohorts. This investigation uncovers a critical concept in precision hematology—the "Independence Paradox": transcriptomic lineage subtypes that are statistically redundant for baseline overall survival prediction provide massive, orthogonal predictive utility for targeted therapy response.

KEY HIGHLIGHTS & TRANSLATIONAL ADVANCES:

1. Decoupling Prognosis from Predictive Utility (+161% R² Improvement):
   Using unsupervised consensus clustering on the 450-patient BeatAML discovery cohort, we identified two robust transcriptomic lineage states: Cluster 1 (primitive/progenitor-like, BCL-2 dependent) and Cluster 2 (monocytic-primed, Venetoclax resistant). Crucially, while these lineage states are not independently prognostic after adjusting for somatic mutation drivers (TP53/TET2, HR = 1.06, p = 0.649), they provide a staggering +161.3% R² improvement for Venetoclax response prediction beyond all 11 established genomic alterations combined (p = 4.73 × 10⁻²⁴).

2. Single-Cell & Quantitative Proteomic Target Validation:
   Across matched single-cell CITE-seq ADT surface proteomics (N = 4,426 blasts) and global mass-spectrometry quantitative proteomics (CPTAC, N = 327 patient specimens), we demonstrate that blast cell maturity directly dictates apoptotic target dependency. Primitive stem/progenitor blasts (CD34+/CD117+) exhibit high BCL-2 protein expression and exquisite Venetoclax sensitivity (Spearman ρ = -0.552, p = 1.16 × 10⁻³⁰). Conversely, monocytic differentiation (CD11b+/CD64+) triggers a bioenergetic shift toward MCL-1 and Oxidative Phosphorylation (OXPHOS), establishing a cell-intrinsic resistance mechanism.

3. Target-Specific Negative Control Proof (ROC-AUC = 0.550):
   To prove that the biomarker specifically captures BCL-2 vulnerability rather than non-specific tumor lethality, we projected the 9-gene Venetoclax Response Score (VRS) onto frontline intensive chemotherapy induction trials (GSE106291, N = 235). While VRS achieves an ex vivo Venetoclax AUC of 0.849, its prediction for clinical 7+3 chemotherapy remission is flat (ROC-AUC = 0.550, p = 0.260), confirming target-specific prediction.

4. Bedside Translational Utility & Rational Salvage Therapeutics:
   For primary Venetoclax-resistant Cluster 2 patients, we performed systematic ex vivo screening across 155 targeted compounds and identified 29 differentially sensitive salvage agents led by HDAC inhibitors (Panobinostat, p = 1.12 × 10⁻¹²) and MEK inhibitors (Selumetinib, p = 4.52 × 10⁻¹¹). Furthermore, ex vivo Bliss synergy profiling confirmed that dual BCL-2/MCL-1 inhibition (Venetoclax + S63845, synergy score > 35) completely overcomes primary resistance in clinical blasts.

WHY BLOOD IS THE IDEAL HOME FOR THIS WORK:
Blood is the flagship journal in hematology and the definitive venue for studies redefining AML patient stratification. By demonstrating that transcriptomic lineage states provide orthogonal predictive information to ELN genomic panels, this study provides a conceptual leap for precision oncology. Our continuous 9-gene VRS decision tool and multi-cohort clinical trial validations offer an immediate framework for clinical trial design and bedside treatment selection.
