Dear Editor,

RE: Original Article Submission - "Molecular Subtypes in Adult Acute Myeloid
Leukemia Predict Venetoclax Response Independent of Genomic Alterations"

We submit for consideration in Nature Medicine our manuscript titled "Molecular
Subtypes in Adult Acute Myeloid Leukemia Predict Venetoclax Response Independent
of Genomic Alterations." This work represents a comprehensive multi-omics
analysis of 2,535 AML patients across three independent cohorts, establishing a
fundamental "Independence Paradox" in cancer biomarkers: molecular subtypes that
are redundant for prognosis but highly independent and superior for treatment
selection.

SIGNIFICANCE AND INNOVATION

Our study provides a transformative framework for precision oncology in AML:

First, we identify and validate two robust transcriptomic subtypes in adult AML.
We demonstrate that these subtypes provide massive independent predictive value
for drug response despite non-independence for prognosis. While our clusters are
statistically redundant with genomic risk factors (TP53, NPM1) for predicting
overall survival (HR=1.06, p=0.649), they provide a staggering +161.3% R²
improvement for Venetoclax response prediction beyond all known genomic
alterations (p=4.7×10⁻²⁴). This finding challenges the current biomarker
paradigm which assumes prognostic indicators are synonymous with predictive ones.

Second, we provide mechanistic and single-cell validation of the "Metabolic
Hybrid" resistant state. We show that Cluster 2 (Venetoclax-resistant) is
characterized by a dual-high OXPHOS/Glycolysis state linked to monocytic
differentiation (d=1.44, p=1.6×10⁻³³). This metabolic reprogramming provides a
cell-intrinsic biological rationale for resistance that is independent of the
mutational landscape.

Third, we establish a clinical roadmap for "Precision Salvage." For the 55% of
patients in the resistant Cluster 2, we identified 8 FDA-approved alternative
therapies led by Panobinostat (d=0.89, p=1.4×10⁻¹¹). Combined with our 9-gene
Venetoclax Response Score (VRS) decision tool, this work offers an immediate
pathway to personalize front-line and salvage therapy in AML.

WHY NATURE MEDICINE

Nature Medicine is the premier venue for this work due to its focus on
transformative translational research. Our study—comprising 2,535 patients, 5
stages of multi-cohort validation, and a clear clinical decision tool—addresses
the "clinical translation gap" in AML. By decoupling prognostic and predictive
utility, we provide a conceptual leap in how transcriptomic profiling should be
integrated into precision medicine. The discovery of the "Independence Paradox"
has implications far beyond AML, suggesting a generalizable strategy for
uncovering therapeutic vulnerabilities in other cancers.

STUDY STRENGTHS

Our manuscript demonstrates several methodological strengths:

• Scale: 2,535 patients across 3 independent cohorts (BeatAML, TCGA, TARGET)

• Rigor: Comprehensive audit-proofing including proportional hazards-free
  survival methods, multivariate decomposition, and study-wide FDR correction.

• Actionability: FDA-approved drug roadmap and a proposed Phase II clinical
  trial design (CLUSTER-AML) provide a clear translational trajectory.
